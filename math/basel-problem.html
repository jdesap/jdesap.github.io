The Basel Problem

June 1, 2025

The Basel problem asks for the precise sum of the reciprocals of the squares of the natural numbers:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \cdots$$

This sum had stumped mathematicians for nearly a century before Euler solved it in 1734, showing the answer is $\frac{\pi^2}{6}$.

Euler's Approach

Euler's key insight was to treat $\sin(x)$ as an infinite polynomial and compare coefficients:

$$\frac{\sin x}{x} = \prod_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(1 - \frac{x^2}{n^2\pi^2}\right)$$

Matching the coefficient of $x^2$ on both sides gives:

$$\boxed{\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}}$$
