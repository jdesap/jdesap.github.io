On Reading Slowly

June 1, 2025

There's a particular pleasure in reading a book so slowly that you forget the plot between sittings. You return to it like a place rather than a story.

I've been reading The Brothers Karamazov this way for three months now. I know Alyosha. I've forgotten what he was doing last chapter. It doesn't seem to matter.

The secret of reading is to know when to be lazy about it.

Most of what I've retained from books is atmosphere rather than argument. The mood of a Chekhov story. The particular weight of time in Proust. I'm not sure this is a failure of attention.
